1. Application overview

StepTracker is a web application that allows a group of users to track their daily step counts, compare progress on leaderboards, and monitor personal fitness statistics over time. It is designed for small to medium groups such as teams, families, or fitness communities.

Step tracking

Log daily step counts for any date. Edit past entries at any time. Progress ring shows how close you are to your daily goal.

Leaderboards

Weekly and monthly rankings show who's walking the most. See total steps, daily averages, and active days for each participant.

Personal stats

View your step trends over time with interactive line charts. Toggle between day, week, month, and year views.

Profiles

Customise your nickname, set a daily step goal, and track height, weight, and target weight.

Admin approval

New user registrations require admin approval before they can access the app, keeping the group private.

Works everywhere

Fully responsive design that works on desktop, tablet, and mobile browsers including iOS Safari.

3. Registration and login

Signing up (new user)

1

Visit the app

Navigate to the StepTracker URL. You'll see the login page.

2

Choose sign-up method

Google SSO: Click "Sign in with Google" and select your Google account. Your name and profile photo are imported automatically.
Email/Password: Click "Register" and fill in your name, email, and password.

3

Wait for approval

You'll see a "Pending Approval" screen. The admin will review and approve your account. Click "Check Status" to refresh, or simply log in again later.

4

Access granted

Once approved, you'll be redirected to the dashboard automatically.

Logging in (existing user)

Use either Google SSO or your email and password on the login page. If you previously signed up with Google, always use the Google button. If you registered with email, use email and password.

4. Dashboard and step logging

The dashboard is the home screen after login. It has three sections:

Yesterday's progress

A circular progress ring shows how many steps you logged yesterday compared to your daily goal. The ring fills as you approach 100% of your goal. If no steps are logged for yesterday, it shows a dash.

This week summary

Four stats cards show your current week's performance:

  • Total Steps: Sum of all steps logged this week
  • Daily Average: Average steps per day (only counting days with entries)
  • Best Day: Highest single-day step count this week
  • Days Logged: Number of days with step entries this week

Logging steps

1

Select a date

The date picker defaults to yesterday. You can select any date up to today. Past dates can be edited at any time.

2

Enter step count

Type your step count for that day. If a previous entry exists, it's pre-filled and shown as "Previously saved: X steps".

3

Save

Click "Save Steps" (or "Update Steps" if editing). The progress ring and week stats update immediately.

6. Leaderboard

The leaderboard ranks all approved users by total step count. Switch between two time periods using the toggle at the top:

  • This Week: Monday to Sunday of the current week
  • This Month: First to last day of the current calendar month

Each entry on the leaderboard shows:

  • Rank position (with medal icons for 1st, 2nd, 3rd place)
  • Profile photo or initials
  • Nickname
  • Total steps for the period
  • Daily average steps
  • Number of active days (days with step entries)

Your own row is highlighted so you can quickly spot your position.

8. Admin panel

The admin panel is accessible only to users with the admin role. It appears as "Admin" in the navigation bar.

Pending users tab

Shows all users with pending status. For each user, the admin can:

  • Approve: Sets the user's status to approved. They can now access the app.
  • Reject: Sets the user's status to rejected. They'll see a "Registration Rejected" message on login.

All users tab

Lists every registered user with their email, status, and role. Useful for reviewing who has access to the application.

1

New user registers

A new entry appears in the "Pending Users" tab.

2

Admin reviews

Open the Admin panel, review the user's name and email.

3

Approve or reject

Click the appropriate button. The user's status updates instantly in Firestore.

4

User gains access

The approved user clicks "Check Status" or logs in again to access the dashboard.

9. Mobile experience

StepTracker is fully responsive and optimised for mobile browsers, including iOS Safari.

  • Navigation: The top bar collapses to a hamburger menu on screens under 768px wide
  • Safe areas: The layout respects iOS notch/home indicator safe areas
  • Touch-friendly: All buttons and inputs are sized for comfortable touch interaction
  • Charts: Line charts resize to fit the screen width and remain interactive on touch
  • Period toggles: Week/month toggle buttons wrap to a second row on narrow screens
Tip: On iOS Safari, you can add StepTracker to your home screen for a more app-like experience. Tap the Share button and choose "Add to Home Screen."
